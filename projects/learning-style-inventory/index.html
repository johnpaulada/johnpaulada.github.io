Assimilator!

  • Strength in inductive reasoning and the ability to create theoretical models
  • Assimilate disparate observations into a meaningful whole / integrated explanation
  • Concerned more with abstract ideas and concepts rather than with people
  • Like their theories to be logically sound and internally consistent, the component ideas need not be practical
  • When plans and theories do not fit, they re-examine the facts
  • Characteristic of people in the basic sciences and mathematics, will do well in planning and research

Converger!

  • Strength in problem-solving, decision-making and practical application of ideas
  • Converge because they do well in situations such as conventional intelligence tests where there is one correct answer or solution to a problem
  • Focus on highly specific problems, solving them through a hypothetical-deductive reasoning approach
  • Tend to be controlled in their expression of emotion
  • Prefer to deal with technical tasks & problems rather than social & interpersonal issues
  • Do well in physical sciences, such as with engineers & technical specialists

Diverger!

  • Strength in imaginative ability and awareness of meaning & values
  • Adapt by viewing concrete situations from different perspectives and organizing different relationships/elements in a meaningful whole or Gestalt
  • Diverge because they do well in situations that call for the generation of alternatives such as brainstorming
  • Adapt by observation rather than action
  • Interested in people and tend to be imaginative & feeling-oriented
  • Characteristic of people in the humanities and liberal arts backgrounds such as counselors, OD Specialists and HR Managers

Accomodator!

  • Strength in doing things, carrying out plans and tasks and getting involved in new experiences
  • Adapt by seeking new opportunities, taking risks and taking action
  • Accommodates changing immediate and emergent circumstances
  • When theories and plans do not fit the facts, they discard the theories and plans
  • Tend to solve problems in an intuitive trial and error method
  • Rely on other people for information rather than on their own analysis
  • At ease with people but can be viewed as impatient and pushy
  • Tends to have an educational background in technical or practical fields such as business
